About Me

My name is Jimmy Opot Alando, and I live in Kenya. I serve as a BYU‑Pathway mentor, guiding students through their academic journeys while encouraging them to discover their potential.

I am passionate about full‑stack web development, currently practicing through projects and internships that focus on the MERN stack. I enjoy building responsive, accessible applications — from course home pages to community‑focused tools like livestock registration and verification systems.

Beyond technology, I am deeply committed to community leadership and development initiatives. My goal is to combine technical skills with social impact, creating solutions that empower individuals and strengthen communities.

When I’m not coding or mentoring, I enjoy exploring biblical and historical studies, engaging in discussions on global power dynamics, and connecting with like‑minded people who share a vision for positive change.
